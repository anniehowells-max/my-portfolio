You've invested in good design. Your website looks considered, professional, and on-brand. So why isn't it converting visitors into enquiries?

More often than not, the answer isn't the design at all. It's the words.

### Design gets people to stay. Copy gets them to act.

When someone lands on your website, your design does the first job — it builds trust, creates a first impression, and signals that you're a credible business. But the moment a visitor wants to understand what you do, who it's for, and why they should contact you, that's where copy takes over.

If your copy is unclear, generic, or written as an afterthought, even the most beautifully designed website will underperform.

### The most common copy mistakes I see

Leading with "we" instead of "you." Many businesses open their homepage talking about themselves — their history, their values, their team. But visitors don't arrive at your site thinking about you. They arrive with a problem they need solving. Good copy acknowledges that immediately.

Vague headlines that say nothing. "Welcome to our website" or "Quality services you can trust" are phrases that appear on thousands of sites and communicate nothing specific. A strong headline tells a visitor exactly what you do and for whom — in plain language.

No clear next step. If a visitor reads your page and doesn't know what to do next, they'll leave. Every page needs a clear call to action, and it needs to feel like a natural next step — not a hard sell.

Copy that was written for the business, not the customer. It's easy to write about what you do in the language you use internally. But your customers often don't know your terminology. Good website copy bridges that gap.

### Where this leaves your designer

If you're working with a web designer — or thinking about hiring one — it's worth knowing that copy and design aren't separate problems. They're deeply connected. The length of a headline affects layout. The tone of your copy shapes how your colour palette and typography feel. A page that hasn't been thought through in terms of content will often need to be redesigned once the copy is finalised.

This is why the most effective website projects start with content strategy, not a blank Figma file.

### What to do about it

You don't necessarily need to hire a professional copywriter (though it can be worth it). What you do need is clarity on three things before your site is built: what your business does, who it's for, and what you want visitors to do. Get those three things nailed, and your designer has something real to work with.

Good design and good copy aren't in competition. When they work together, that's when websites actually perform.
